Do You Want to Increase your Lifespan?

     In the 1500s, explorer Ponce de Leon searched around the world for the Fountain of Youth. And today, the increase in cosmetic surgery shows the human desire to stay young and delay aging. With new scientific technology, today's researchers are studying genetics for clues about longevity, or living a long life. Already, just by modifying one gene, scientists have been able to double the lifespan for groups of fruit flies and roundworms. And the results of other animal experiments seem very promising. But isn't aging a natural part of human life? Scientists should work in genetics to end disease, not increase our lifespan.
