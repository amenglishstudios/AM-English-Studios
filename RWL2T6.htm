"Language Styles in the Workplace"

     Deborah Tannen is a sociolinguist who studies communication styles between men and women. Tannen reports that men and women use language in different ways. She says generally men use conversations as an opportunity to gain power. They do not want to look weak. But women think that language and conversation is a way to connect to other people. These communication differences can cause misunderstandings in the workplace.

     Tannen reports that women use more indirect language than men. For example, a female boss may say, "Maybe it would be a good idea to transfer this file to Joe's computer. "A female worker understands that the boss has asked her to transfer a file. However, a male worker may think the boss gave him a choice. He might think it was something could do, but he didn't have to.

     On the other hand, men use more direct language than women. A male boss might tell a male worker," Transfer this file to Joe's computer." The male worker understands the request and feels comfortable with it. But a female worker might think the boss is forcing her to do something and not being polite.

     Clearly there are differences in the way men and women communicate. Men use more direct language, and women use more indirect language. And these differences cause misunderstandings. But Tannen believes if both men and women recognize their differences, they could develop better strategies of how to communicate better with each other.
