INSTRUCTIONS: Read this introduction for a descriptive essay and answer the questions.

Jim Jones and the People's Temple

     In 1956, Jim Jones started his own church called the People's Temple. Jones said he wanted to build a society without racism and where all economic levels were equal. He had a charismatic, powerful personality and he convinced church members to give up all their possessions and give them to the church. But in addition to these goals, he also said he was God. He did whatever he wanted with the members' property. If anyone criticized him, they were punished. Because Jones began to get negative reports in the newspapers, he decided to move his church to Guyana, a small country in South America. While the church members were there, Jones revealed more of his desire for power, control and destruction.
