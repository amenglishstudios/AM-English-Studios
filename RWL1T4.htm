"Frederick Douglass: From a Slave to Freedom"

     Frederick Douglass was born a slave, but his great courage and desire for freedom made him admired throughout the country. In 1818, Douglass was born into slavery in the southern state of Maryland. He had to work on a farm long hours and was punished severely if he didn't obey the slave owners. Douglass wanted to be free, and when he was 20 years old, he successfully escaped on a train to New York. Douglass wanted to help other slaves, and he became an important leader in the Anti-Slavery Movement. He spoke all over the country about ending slavery. He became very famous and President Abraham Lincoln asked him to be one of his Presidential advisers.
