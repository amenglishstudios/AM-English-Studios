INSTRUCTIONS: Read the essay and answer the questions that follow.

Computer Technology in Society

          The advent of the computer age changed the lives of people around the globe. Generally, people would agree that the presence of computer technology has benefited people's lifestyles for the better. Although computer technology has benefited society as a whole, it has also resulted in some notable differences between the lifestyles of today and the lifestyles of twenty years ago.
          Before computer technology became a common part of any business office, life at work proceeded rather slowly. Communication was sent via the postal service, and people who did not answer their phones were unreachable. In contrast, modern-day business people communicate via fax, e-mail, and voicemail, and they are accessible virtually twenty-four hours a day. Although most people see this increased availability as a benefit, there are some who would argue that it detracts from personal freedom and it infringes on people's private time. People rarely say, "The letter's in the mail" anymore; with fax machines, the letter should immediately be on its way---with no excuses allowed.
          In addition to technology's providing more speed in business offices, it increases the number of people any given person has access to. Before the use of technology became so common, people only communicated with others whom they had already met. In contrast to earlier days, the average person has extraordinary, immediate access to people around the globe. Even though most people see this as a benefit, a clear drawback is that all the people a person has access to in turn have access to that person. People who are concerned about privacy or security may consider this difference to be a less than positive one.
          Likewise, some people are concerned about another change that computer technology has brought to society. Before this advanced technology, children had access to little more information than their parents and teachers provided them. Certainly, their friends were sources of input, but in general, the information was relatively limited. Unlike the children of twenty years ago, children these days can get information from an unlimited number of sources. In general, this is viewed as a positive aspect of technology; on the other hand, there are parents who are concerned about what exactly their children will be exposed to.
          As a whole, computer technology has improved the lives of many people by making their communication faster and more abundant and by giving them access to a world of information. However, it still remains to be seen if the potential benefits do in fact outweigh the drawbacks.
